process

1. **Demands for Grants

![[Budget_preparation_infographic_a276de20eb.jpeg]]

Once the Finance Ministry receives the consolidated estimates, it categorizes and scrutinizes them. Expenditure estimates are classified into three types, each subject to a different level of scrutiny:
Type of Charge Description Level of Scrutiny by Finance Ministry
1. Standing Charges Permanent, fixed liabilities (e.g., salaries, permanent establishment). Minimum Scrutiny (Usually approved automatically).
2. Continuing Charges Ongoing projects or temporary establishment costs. Moderate Scrutiny.
3. New Schemes Proposals for entirely new developmental schemes or projects. Maximum/Rigorous Scrutiny.
  • Dispute Resolution: If a disagreement arises between the Administrative Ministry (demanding funds) and the Finance Ministry (controlling funds), the matter is escalated to the Cabinet. The Cabinet’s decision is binding and final.

** 2. Revenue Estimation & Tax Structuring**

Once the Expenditure Estimates are locked, the focus shifts to calculating the income required to fund them.

  • Responsible Entities: The Department of Revenue, acting jointly with the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) and the Central Board of Excise and Customs (CBEC) [Now CBIC], forecasts the expected revenue for the upcoming year.

  • Methodology: Forecasts are fundamentally based on past revenue collection trends.

  • Tax Adjustments: Based on the total expenditure requirements, the Finance Ministry decides whether to alter (increase/decrease) existing tax rates to meet the deficit.

  • Policy Mandate: Any major policy-level decision regarding taxation is strictly taken by the Cabinet.

F. Final Output for Parliament

Following the successful compilation of both Revenue and Expenditure data, the Finance Ministry drafts the two primary constitutional documents to be tabled in Parliament:

  1. [[#1. **Demands for Grants]] Details the comprehensive Expenditure requirements of various ministries.

  2. Annual Financial Statement: The core macro-document summarizing the overall Revenue and Expenditure of the Government.

     →→ Vote of Credit (Blank Cheque)

     →→ Token Grant (Reappropriation)
